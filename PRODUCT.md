# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients evaluating a solo engineer for paid project work: startup founders, product leads, and agency owners arriving from Upwork, LinkedIn, or referrals. They scan fast (seconds, not minutes), are burned by template portfolios, and need to answer two questions immediately: "what exactly does this person build?" and "can I trust them with money?" Secondary audience: technical hiring managers and recruiters.

## Product Purpose

Convert visitors into project inquiries for Jay Dhakan, a solo Python & AI/ML engineer based in India (LLM/RAG chatbots, multi-agent automation, data pipelines at scale, high-performance APIs, full-stack web apps). Success = qualified contact-form submissions and direct outreach. The site itself is the engineering proof: Lighthouse 95+ performance is a hard gate, not a nice-to-have.

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, direct. "Boutique solo developer: focused output, no fluff, direct communication. No middlemen, no handoffs." Premium, editorial, and alive: dark, indigo-accented, SaaS-product feel (Linear/Vercel lineage), with motion that proves craft rather than decorates. First-person voice throughout.

## Anti-references

- Generic freelancer templates and theme-marketplace portfolios (uniform card dumps, stock illustrations, resume-as-webpage).
- Heavy 3D spectacle (Bruno Simon-style worlds): the only 3D allowed is one lightweight hero shader that never competes with text or Lighthouse.
- Agency-speak and filler verbs ("elevate", "seamless", "unleash"); fake-precise invented metrics.
- Anything that reads "AI made this": effect-stacking, purple-gradient slop, dead placeholder content.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The site is the case study: performance, accessibility, and polish are the proof of engineering quality.
2. **Speed to answer.** Positioning, proof, and a contact path within the first viewport and at every scroll break.
3. **Real numbers or no numbers.** Every metric comes from the resume or real client work; nothing invented.
4. **Motion as proof, not decoration.** "Code as craft, motion as proof": every animation is purposeful, springy, and respects reduced motion.
5. **One signature idea.** A living indigo shader hero + obsessive micro-interaction craft; restraint everywhere else.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast minimum (4.5:1 body text), Lighthouse 100 Accessibility target. Every animation gated behind `prefers-reduced-motion` via `useReducedMotion()`. Full keyboard navigability with visible focus rings, skip-to-content link, labeled form inputs, `aria-label` on icon-only buttons, decorative elements (cursor, marquee) `aria-hidden`. Custom cursor disabled on touch devices and under reduced motion.
